About the project

A one-year initiative building lasting change

This project builds an interdisciplinary Community of Practice (CoP) — a group spanning disciplines, faculties, and strategic centres of the University of Bern. Together, the network develops an inter- and transdisciplinary working model addressing the opportunities and challenges of digitalisation in higher education, with a focus on Generation Alpha.

The CoP's findings and recommendations are consolidated in a White Paper. To turn insight into lasting impact, the network will submit a collaborative research grant (Vernetzungsantrag) to the University of Bern in autumn 2026 — potentially funding up to CHF 3 million over four years.

The focus

Three questions at the core

01

The challenge

Generation Alpha (born 2010–2025) grows up with constant access to technology — yet may face a critical gap in the digital competencies needed for learning, problem-solving, and decision-making. Universities, too, face under-developed digital teaching, research, and administrative processes.

02

The approach

An interdisciplinary network combines literature review with empirical research to fully understand digitalisation at UniBE. Through collaborative writing across two semesters and a two-day retreat, the CoP co-creates a shared working model and White Paper.

03

The goal

To develop and evaluate digital methods for teaching, research, and administration that work across disciplines — fostering both technical skills and essential human capacities: self-regulation, digital well-being, critical thinking, and digital ethics.
